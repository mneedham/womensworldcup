Preliminaries

Africa

Africa

The 2018 CAF Women's Africa Cup of Nations took place between 17 November and 1 December in Ghana, where Nigeria, South Africa and Cameroon claimed the continent's three qualification places for the FIFA Women's World Cup France 2019.

Qualified teams
  • Cameroon CMR
  • Nigeria NGA
  • South Africa RSA

Asia

Asia

The 2018 AFC Asian Women's Cup took place in Jordan, where China PR, Thailand, Australia and Japan reached the semi-finals and sealed their spots at the FIFA Women's World Cup France 2019. In the match for fifth place, Korea Republic secured the continent's fifth and final qualifying berth.

Qualified teams
  • Australia AUS
  • China PR CHN
  • Japan JPN
  • Korea Republic KOR
  • Thailand THA

Europe

Europe

After the first qualifying round (6-11 April 2017), the remaining 35 teams were divided into seven groups of five.

Italy, Spain, England, Germany, Norway, Scotland and Sweden qualified as group winners to join hosts France. The Netherlands beat Switzerland and Denmark in play-offs to claim Europe's final berth.

Qualified teams
  • England ENG
  • France FRA
  • Germany GER
  • Italy ITA
  • Netherlands NED
  • Norway NOR
  • Scotland SCO
  • Spain ESP
  • Sweden SWE

North, Central America and Caribbean

North, Central America and Caribbean

USA, Canada and Jamaica won their tickets to France 2019 at the 2018 CONCACAF Women's Championship, which took place between 4 and 17 October 2018 in USA. Fourth-placed Panama were defeated by Argentina in the intercontinental play-off in November 2018.

Qualified teams
  • Canada CAN
  • Jamaica JAM
  • USA USA

Oceania

Oceania

Hosts Fiji advanced from the preliminary round (25-31 August) to join the eight-nation field for the 2018 OFC Women's Nations Cup

New Zealand won the tournament held between 18 November - 1 December 2018 in New Caledonia to qualify for France 2019.

Qualified teams
  • New Zealand NZL

South America

South America

The Copa America Femenina 2018 was held between 4-22 April in Chile. Brazil and Chile finished top-two to qualify for France 2019, while third-placed Argentina also booked their place after beating the fourth-placed CONCACAF side Panama in a play-off in November 2018.

Qualified teams
  • Argentina ARG
  • Brazil BRA
  • Chile CHI
